        <hr>

        

        <h2>Week 1: Establishing the Baseline</h2>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Saturday (Day 1): Moderate Climbing Session</h3>

            

            <p><strong>Warm-up:</strong> 10–15 minutes of "arcing" (easy, continuous movement on large holds) and tendon glides [8, 9].</p>

            

            <p><strong>Climbing Activity:</strong></p>

            <ul>

                <li><strong>Intensity:</strong> One full grade below your on-sight level [10].</li>

                <li><strong>Terrain:</strong> Vertical to mildly overhanging.</li>

                <li><strong>Holds:</strong> Half crimps are permitted on large edges; avoid full crimping or high-velocity dynos [10, 11].</li>

            </ul>

            

            <p><strong>Core:</strong> <strong>Floor Session</strong> (3 sets of 8–12 reps): Leg tucks, Copenhagen planks (each side), and back arches [12, 13].</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Sunday (Day 2): Mobility & Recovery</h3>

            

            <ul>

                <li><strong>Tendon Glides:</strong> 5–10 minutes.</li>

                <li><strong>IASTM:</strong> Use a light tool (like a butter knife) for soft tissue mobilization over the pulley to reorganize scar tissue [14].</li>

                <li><strong>Shoulder Stability:</strong> Cuban Rotations (3 sets of 12 reps) with light weights to build external rotation strength [15, 16].</li>

            </ul>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Monday (Day 3): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day</h3>

            

            <ol>

                <li><strong>Warm-up:</strong> Tendon glides and full-body movement.</li>

                <li><strong>Compound Movement (The Anabolic Trigger):</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li><strong>Deadlifts:</strong> 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.</li>

                        <li><strong>Push-ups & Pull-ups:</strong> 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.</li>

                        <li><strong>Rest:</strong> Only <strong>30–60 seconds</strong> between sets to maintain hormone spikes [17].</li>

                    </ul>

                </li>

                <li><strong>Finger Rehab (Immediately After Trigger):</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li><strong>Protocol:</strong> Repeaters (7s on, 3s off). 7 reps per set, 3 sets total [18].</li>

                        <li><strong>Rest:</strong> 3 minutes between sets.</li>

                        <li><strong>Grip:</strong> Half Crimp (at your 50lb baseline) and Open Hand.</li>

                    </ul>

                </li>

                <li><strong>Core:</strong> <strong>Suspension Session</strong> (3–4 sets of 3–5 reps): Prone "I" and Supine "I" on rings or TRX [19, 20].</li>

            </ol>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Tuesday (Day 4): Mobility & Recovery</h3>

            

            <p>Tendon glides, IASTM, and 15–30 minutes of light stretching or yoga [8, 21].</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Wednesday (Day 5): Moderate Climbing Session</h3>

            

            <p>Same as Day 1. Focus on technical "curiosity" and experimenting with different foot positions to reduce hand load [22, 23].</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Thursday (Day 6): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day</h3>

            

            <p>Repeat the Monday (Day 3) protocol: <strong>Compound Lifts → Finger Repeaters → Core (Floor Session)</strong>.</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Friday (Day 7): Full Rest</h3>

            

            <p>No training. Focus on <strong>7–9 hours of sleep</strong> and a high-protein, anti-inflammatory diet [24, 25].</p>

        </div>

        

        <hr>

        

        <h2>Week 2: Controlled Progression</h2>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Saturday (Day 8): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day</h3>

            

            <ol>

                <li><strong>Compound Lifts:</strong> Deadlifts, push-ups, and pull-ups (8–12 reps, 30–60s rest) [17].</li>

                <li><strong>Finger Training:</strong> Stage 2 Repeaters.

                    <ul>

                        <li><em>Progression:</em> If 50 lbs has felt effortlessly pain-free, add <strong>2–5 lbs</strong> to the load, provided pain remains at <strong>0/10</strong> [26, 27].</li>

                    </ul>

                </li>

                <li><strong>Core:</strong> <strong>Suspension Session</strong> (3–4 sets of 3–5 reps) [20].</li>

            </ol>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Sunday (Day 9): Mobility & Tissue Work</h3>

            

            <p>IASTM massage, tendon glides, and Cuban Rotations [14, 15].</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Monday (Day 10): Transition to "Hard" Climbing</h3>

            

            <ul>

                <li><strong>Intensity:</strong> You may climb up to your <strong>on-sight level</strong> [28].</li>

                <li><strong>Terrain:</strong> Vertical walls only.</li>

                <li><strong>Grip:</strong> Crimps are allowed, but maintain strictly controlled movement [11, 28].</li>

                <li><strong>Rule:</strong> If you feel even a 1/10 pain response, immediately drop back to "Moderate" difficulty [29].</li>

            </ul>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Tuesday (Day 11): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day</h3>

            

            <p>Repeat the Strength protocol: <strong>Compound Lifts → Finger Repeaters (current weight) → Core (Floor Session)</strong>.</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Wednesday (Day 12): Mobility & Recovery</h3>

            

            <p>Tendon glides and light "arcing" (15 mins) for active recovery [8, 30].</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Thursday (Day 13): Moderate/Hard Climbing</h3>

            

            <p>Climbing session focusing on <strong>body tension</strong> (climbing without cutting feet) [31]. Use H-taping and the Rule of 7 [1, 5].</p>

        </div>

        

        <div class="day-section">

            <h3>Friday (Day 14): Final Assessment & Rest</h3>

            

            <p>Complete rest. If you have maintained <strong>0/10 pain</strong> through all loads and climbing, you are ready to transition out of the "No Pain" rehab phase and into high-intensity loading [27, 32].</p>

        </div>
