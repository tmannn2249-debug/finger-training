Week 1: Establishing the Baseline

Saturday (Day 1): Moderate Climbing Session
▼

Warm-up: 10–15 minutes of "arcing" (easy, continuous movement on large holds) and tendon glides [8, 9].

Climbing Activity:

  • Intensity: One full grade below your on-sight level [10].
  • Terrain: Vertical to mildly overhanging.
  • Holds: Half crimps are permitted on large edges; avoid full crimping or high-velocity dynos [10, 11].

Core: Floor Session (3 sets of 8–12 reps): Leg tucks, Copenhagen planks (each side), and back arches [12, 13].

Sunday (Day 2): Mobility & Recovery
▼
  • Tendon Glides: 5–10 minutes.
  • IASTM: Use a light tool (like a butter knife) for soft tissue mobilization over the pulley to reorganize scar tissue [14].
  • Shoulder Stability: Cuban Rotations (3 sets of 12 reps) with light weights to build external rotation strength [15, 16].
Monday (Day 3): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day
▼
  1. Warm-up: Tendon glides and full-body movement.
  2. Compound Movement (The Anabolic Trigger):
    • Deadlifts: 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.
    • Push-ups & Pull-ups: 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.
    • Rest: Only 30–60 seconds between sets to maintain hormone spikes [17].
  3. Finger Rehab (Immediately After Trigger):
    • Protocol: Repeaters (7s on, 3s off). 7 reps per set, 3 sets total [18].
    • Rest: 3 minutes between sets.
    • Grip: Half Crimp (at your 50lb baseline) and Open Hand.
  4. Core: Suspension Session (3–4 sets of 3–5 reps): Prone "I" and Supine "I" on rings or TRX [19, 20].
Tuesday (Day 4): Mobility & Recovery
▼

Tendon glides, IASTM, and 15–30 minutes of light stretching or yoga [8, 21].

Wednesday (Day 5): Moderate Climbing Session
▼

Same as Day 1. Focus on technical "curiosity" and experimenting with different foot positions to reduce hand load [22, 23].

Thursday (Day 6): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day
▼

Repeat the Monday (Day 3) protocol: Compound Lifts → Finger Repeaters → Core (Floor Session).

Friday (Day 7): Full Rest
▼

No training. Focus on 7–9 hours of sleep and a high-protein, anti-inflammatory diet [24, 25].

Week 2: Controlled Progression

Saturday (Day 8): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day
▼
  1. Compound Lifts: Deadlifts, push-ups, and pull-ups (8–12 reps, 30–60s rest) [17].
  2. Finger Training: Stage 2 Repeaters.
    • Progression: If 50 lbs has felt effortlessly pain-free, add 2–5 lbs to the load, provided pain remains at 0/10 [26, 27].
  3. Core: Suspension Session (3–4 sets of 3–5 reps) [20].
Sunday (Day 9): Mobility & Tissue Work
▼

IASTM massage, tendon glides, and Cuban Rotations [14, 15].

Monday (Day 10): Transition to "Hard" Climbing
▼
  • Intensity: You may climb up to your on-sight level [28].
  • Terrain: Vertical walls only.
  • Grip: Crimps are allowed, but maintain strictly controlled movement [11, 28].
  • Rule: If you feel even a 1/10 pain response, immediately drop back to "Moderate" difficulty [29].
Tuesday (Day 11): Strength & Hormonal Trigger Day
▼

Repeat the Strength protocol: Compound Lifts → Finger Repeaters (current weight) → Core (Floor Session).

Wednesday (Day 12): Mobility & Recovery
▼

Tendon glides and light "arcing" (15 mins) for active recovery [8, 30].

Thursday (Day 13): Moderate/Hard Climbing
▼

Climbing session focusing on body tension (climbing without cutting feet) [31]. Use H-taping and the Rule of 7 [1, 5].

Friday (Day 14): Final Assessment & Rest
▼

Complete rest. If you have maintained 0/10 pain through all loads and climbing, you are ready to transition out of the "No Pain" rehab phase and into high-intensity loading [27, 32].
